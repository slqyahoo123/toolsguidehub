## üîç 1. Identify Your Subscriptions

Before you can cancel, you need to find them. 
- **Check Your Credit Card Statement:** Search for recurring transactions.
- **Email Audit:** Search your inbox for "Invoice," "Receipt," or "Your subscription."
- **App Store/Google Play:** Check your mobile device's subscription manager (Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions).

## üõ†Ô∏è 2. Step-by-Step Cancellation for Popular Platforms

### Adobe Creative Cloud
Adobe is notorious for "Early Termination Fees."
1. Log in to your Adobe account.
2. Go to **Manage Plan**.
3. Select **Cancel your plan**.
4. **Pro Tip:** Sometimes if you choose "It's too expensive," Adobe will offer you 2-3 months for free to stay.

### Microsoft 365
1. Go to the **Services & Subscriptions** page of your Microsoft account dashboard.
2. Find your subscription and select **Manage**.
3. Select **Cancel subscription** or **Turn off recurring billing**.

### Slack
1. Only Workspace Owners can cancel.
2. Go to the **Administration** > **Billing** settings.
3. Select the **Menu** icon next to your plan and choose **Cancel**.

## üõ°Ô∏è 3. Dealing with "Dark Patterns"

Companies often use these tricks to stop you from cancelling:
- **The "Hidden Button":** Making the "Cancel" button a tiny light gray link while the "Keep Plan" button is a big bright blue.
- **The "Call Us" Requirement:** Some legacy SaaS platforms force you to talk to a human to cancel. In the EU and some US states (like California), this is becoming illegal if you signed up online.
- **The "Survey Loop":** Forcing you to answer 5-10 questions before finally reaching the cancellation confirmation.

**Action Plan:** If you find a site intentionally making it impossible to cancel, use a **Virtual Credit Card** (like Privacy.com or Revolut) to pause the card, or contest the charge with your bank.
