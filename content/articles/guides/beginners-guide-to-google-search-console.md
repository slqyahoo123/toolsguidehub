You could have the most beautiful website in the world, but if Google doesn't know it exists, you'll get zero organic traffic. **Google Search Console (GSC)** is the free, official diagnostic tool provided by Google to help webmasters understand their site's search performance.

In 2026, it's more powerful than ever. This guide walks you through setup, core features, and the actionable insights you should check every week.

## ğŸ› ï¸ 1. Setting Up Search Console

### Step 1: Verify Your Domain
- Go to [search.google.com/search-console](https://search.google.com/search-console).
- Add your property using the "Domain" method (recommended) or the "URL Prefix" method.
- Verify ownership via DNS record (most reliable), HTML file upload, or your Google Analytics tag.

### Step 2: Submit Your Sitemap
- Navigate to **Sitemaps** in the left-hand menu.
- Enter your sitemap URL (e.g., `yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml`).
- Click **Submit**.

This is the single most important action for getting your pages indexed quickly.

## ğŸ“Š 2. The Dashboard: What to Check Weekly

### Performance Report
This shows you the queries that bring users to your site.
- **Impressions:** How many times your page appeared in search results.
- **Clicks:** How many times users actually clicked on your result.
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate):** Clicks / Impressions. If this is below 2%, your title/description might need optimization.
- **Average Position:** Where your page ranks on average. Anything on Page 1 (positions 1-10) is gold.

### Coverage Report
This shows which of your pages Google has successfully indexed.
- **Valid Pages:** Google found and indexed them. Good.
- **Excluded Pages:** Google found them but chose not to index them. This requires investigation.
- **Errors:** A critical issue is preventing indexing. Fix these immediately.

## ğŸ¯ 3. The "Inspect URL" Tool

This is the most underrated feature in GSC. Paste any URL from your site and Google will tell you:
1. Whether the page is indexed.
2. When it was last crawled.
3. Whether there are any mobile usability issues.
4. The detected canonical URL.

**Pro Tip:** After publishing a new article, use "Request Indexing" on the URL Inspection tool to ask Google to crawl it immediately instead of waiting days.

## ğŸ“ˆ 4. Core Web Vitals Report

Since 2021, Google uses page speed as a ranking factor. The GSC "Core Web Vitals" report shows you which pages pass or fail on three metrics:
- **LCP (Largest Contentful Paint):** How fast the main content loads (aim for under 2.5 seconds).
- **INP (Interaction to Next Paint):** How responsive the page is to user interactions (aim for under 200ms).
- **CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift):** How much content "jumps around" during load (aim for under 0.1).
